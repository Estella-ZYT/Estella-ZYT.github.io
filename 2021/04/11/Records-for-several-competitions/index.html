Records about several competitions

Posted on 2021-04-11 | |

About AMC10 and 12

I attended AMC10 on February 5 and AMC12 on February 11. Something unexpected through AMC10 was that I was too nervous and couldn’t think of methods to work out the questions, or even understand what they were asking for. As a result, I answered only about 15 questions (25 questions in all), which was far below my normal performance. In fact, I often worry about not getting good grades and thus feel anxious and tense before exams, but none was as serious as this time. I know that it’s necessary to keep a relaxed mind in such situations but it’s just hard for me to control my emotions. Anyway, I will try to avoid being too nervous next time.

I performed normally in AMC12, though there were still many questions left unanswered. My grade was only 1.5 points to enter AIME and it was really a pity, but that was not out of expectation. I was not familiar with the mathematical formulae and theorems needed for the competitions, and those are what I need to work on this year to gain a better understanding so that I may achieve a higher grades next time.

AMC contains 25 multiple choice questions but only lasts for 75 minutes, which means each question takes only 3 minutes on average. However, some of the questions are supper difficult and require more knowledge than we learn at school. Though I attended extra-curriculum courses on AMC, I still didn’t understand the knowledge well and had difficulty in putting them into practice.

About Physics Bowl

I have taken courses to prepare for the competition since October. This competition includes 40 multiple choice questions and lasts for only 45 minutes. However, the contents to be examined are very extensive and detailed, even more than what we can learn in A-level and IB courses. Therefore, finishing all the test paper in the limited time becomes much more difficult. Though I may know the methods and formulae needed and be able to work out the problems, I couldn’t attain this in such short time. In my view, the amount of knowledge required for the Physics Bowl was too massive for me to grasp even though I did lots of exercises, and all I could achieve was to remember the formulae and laws to the greatest extent and try to use them in the competition. I don’t think I will get a satisfying grade because I didn’t answer as many questions as I did in the mock, but I still think this experience is rewarding, for physics is intriguing and it will also make me learn this subject more easily in the following semesters.

About Euclid

Different from the two competitions above, Euclid Contest consists of 10 questions including both short answer ones and full solution ones. Steps to work out the questions must be presented clearly on the answer sheet.

I thought the time was sufficient (2.5 hours), but writing the detailed solution took a lot of time and I didn’t have enough time to think deeply about the difficult ones at last. I still cannot determine whether it was wise to spend such long time on the questions which I was confident to answer correctly. I should have asked about the mark scheme before the contest.

The clearest feeling is that my knowledge is not board enough and I cannot use what I learnt skillfully to solve problems, which have a greater effect on the exams where the time is limited. I think the best way to enhance the mastery of knowledge is to do more exercises, and I will work on it through the following year. Besides, some of the contents will be contained in courses at school as well. I believe being more prepared the next time will lead to better results.
